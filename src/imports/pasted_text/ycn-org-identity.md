  WHO WE ARE  

Youth Climate Network (YCN) is a grassroots, youth-led non-profit organization established in Bangladesh and operating at the forefront of climate justice and environmental advocacy across Asia Pacific and South Asia. Founded by young people, for young people, YCN embodies the conviction that the generation most affected by the climate crisis must also lead the response to it.

We work at the intersection of climate science, community resilience, social equity, and youth empowerment — recognizing that environmental challenges are inseparable from issues of poverty, gender inequality, food security, and human dignity. YCN bridges the gap between local grassroots action and regional and global policy platforms, ensuring that the voices of frontline youth communities are heard, respected, and acted upon.

Officially registered under the Department of Youth Development, Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh (Registration No: DYD/Khulna/Reg-104), YCN operates with full legal standing and institutional accountability. We are committed to transparency, community ownership, and impact-driven programming that leaves no one behind.

Our Vision

  A climate-resilient, environmentally just, and sustainably developed South Asia and Asia Pacific — where every young person has the knowledge, power, and opportunity to shape a thriving planet for present and future generations.

Our Mission

Youth Climate Network mobilizes, equips, and amplifies the power of young people to drive transformative action on climate change, environmental justice, and sustainable development. We build the capacity of youth leaders and frontline communities; advocate for systemic change at local, national, and regional levels; deliver life-changing programs in education, livelihoods, water, sanitation, disaster preparedness, and renewable energy; and foster inclusive, collaborative networks that leave no community behind.

Core Values

  STRATEGIC FOCUS AREAS & PROGRAMS  

YCN's work spans six interconnected strategic pillars, each addressing a dimension of climate vulnerability and sustainable development:

1. Climate Justice & Environmental Advocacy
YCN places climate justice at the heart of everything we do. Bangladesh is among the world's most climate-vulnerable nations — facing rising sea levels, cyclones, floods, salinity intrusion, and extreme heat — yet contributes less than 0.5% of global emissions. We advocate fiercely and unapologetically for the rights of affected communities.

Key Programs:
•	Regional Climate Advocacy — Representing Bangladesh and South Asian youth at UN climate negotiations (UNFCCC/COP), Asia Pacific regional forums, and international environmental justice platforms.
•	Environmental Legal Literacy — Educating communities on their environmental rights under national law and international frameworks.
•	Biodiversity Conservation Campaigns — Community-based mangrove restoration, wetland protection, and biodiversity monitoring in the Sundarbans and coastal belt.
•	Anti-Pollution Awareness — Campaigns targeting industrial pollution, plastic contamination, and air quality degradation.
•	Climate Litigation Support — Assisting frontline communities in documenting climate harms for advocacy and legal proceedings.

2. Education, Research & Knowledge Management
Education is both a right and a tool for transformation. YCN builds climate literacy and critical thinking from the classroom to the community hall, while producing original research that informs national and regional policy.

Key Programs:
•	Climate & Environment Education in Schools — Integrating climate change modules into school curricula in partnership with local education boards.
•	Youth Research Fellowship — Annual fellowships supporting young researchers to conduct community-based climate and environmental studies.
•	Climate Research Assistance — Supporting academic institutions and NGOs with field data collection, community surveys, and participatory research.
•	Knowledge Exchange Platform — Annual symposiums, publications, and digital platforms to share findings with policymakers and practitioners.
•	Public Awareness Campaigns — Mass media, social media, and street campaigns on climate change, biodiversity, and environmental health.

3. Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)
Safe water and dignified sanitation are non-negotiable human rights. Climate change is intensifying water stress across Bangladesh and the wider region, disproportionately harming women, children, and coastal communities. YCN's WASH programs deliver tangible, life-changing improvements in water security and sanitary conditions.

Key Programs:
•	Safe Drinking Water Access — Installation and maintenance of arsenic-free tube wells, rainwater harvesting systems, and community water purification units.
•	Sanitation Infrastructure — Construction of climate-resilient latrines and hygiene facilities in flood-prone and coastal areas.
•	Menstrual Hygiene Management — Providing products, education, and safe facilities for girls and women in schools and communities.
•	WASH in Schools — Ensuring every partner school meets minimum WASH standards.
•	Community Hygiene Education — Behavior change communication on handwashing, open defecation-free communities, and waterborne disease prevention.

4. Disaster Risk Management, Relief & Resilience
Living on the frontlines of climate change means facing disasters with increasing frequency and intensity. YCN works with communities to anticipate, prepare, respond to, and recover from climate-induced disasters — building a culture of resilience rather than dependency.

Key Programs:
•	Community Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) Training — Training youth volunteers and community leaders in early warning systems, evacuation planning, and emergency response.
•	Youth Disaster Response Teams — Rapid-response volunteer brigades equipped and trained for flood, cyclone, and storm surge emergencies.
•	Relief Distribution — Coordinating timely distribution of food, clean water, medicines, and non-food items in disaster-affected areas.
•	Post-Disaster Psychosocial Support — Providing mental health first aid and community counseling in the aftermath of disasters.
•	Climate Risk Mapping — Participatory mapping of climate hazards and vulnerabilities at the community level to guide planning.

 
5. Sustainable Livelihoods & Resilience-Based Agriculture
Climate change is destroying traditional livelihoods — sinking farmlands, increasing salinity, displacing fishing communities, and disrupting seasonal patterns. YCN helps affected communities transition to and strengthen climate-adaptive livelihoods and sustainable food systems.

Key Programs:
•	Resilience-Based Agriculture — Training farmers in climate-smart techniques including saline-tolerant crop varieties, raised-bed farming, floating gardens, and water-efficient irrigation.
•	Alternative Livelihood Development — Supporting transitions from climate-vulnerable livelihoods (e.g., brackish water fishing, flood-prone subsistence farming) to diversified, sustainable income sources.
•	Agroforestry & Food Security — Integrating trees into farming landscapes to improve food diversity, income, and ecosystem services.
•	Market Linkage & Value Chains — Connecting smallholder farmers and producers with fair markets, cooperatives, and digital trading platforms.
•	Homestead Gardening for Nutrition — Promoting kitchen gardens among women and households for nutritional security and income generation.

6. Renewable Energy & Waste Management
Bangladesh's energy future must be clean, affordable, and just. YCN promotes renewable energy access in underserved communities while tackling the growing crisis of plastic and electronic waste threatening ecosystems and human health.

Key Programs:
•	Solar Energy for Rural Communities — Facilitating access to solar home systems, solar-powered irrigation, and mini-grids for off-grid households and institutions.
•	Biogas Programs — Supporting biogas digester installation for clean cooking and organic waste management in rural households.
•	Plastic Waste Reduction Campaigns — Community-based campaigns and school programs targeting single-use plastic elimination.
•	Community Waste Management Systems — Supporting local governments and communities to establish segregated waste collection and composting systems.
•	E-Waste Awareness — Education and safe disposal campaigns for electronic waste in urban and peri-urban areas.

7. Tree Plantation & Ecosystem Restoration
Trees are among nature's most powerful climate solutions. YCN's tree plantation programs combine ecological restoration with community empowerment, creating green infrastructure that benefits both the environment and livelihoods.

Key Programs:
•	National and Regional Tree Plantation Drives — Annual campaigns planting native, climate-resilient tree species across schools, roadsides, riverbanks, and degraded lands.
•	Mangrove Restoration — Community-led mangrove replanting in the Sundarbans buffer zone and coastal embankments for storm protection and carbon sequestration.
•	Urban Greening — School and community urban greening projects in cities to reduce heat island effects and improve air quality.
•	Social Forestry — Supporting community ownership and management of planted forests for sustainable livelihoods.

8. Youth Capacity Development & Leadership
YCN invests deeply in building the next generation of climate leaders. We believe that young people equipped with skills, knowledge, and networks can drive systemic change in their communities and beyond.

Key Programs:
•	Youth Leadership Academy — Intensive multi-module leadership training program for young activists, community organizers, and emerging leaders.
•	Climate Negotiation & Advocacy Training — Preparing youth delegates for participation in national and international climate forums.
•	Vocational & Skills Training — Technical and vocational education in renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, environmental monitoring, and social work.
•	Digital Skills & Communication — Training young people in digital advocacy, social media campaigning, data visualization, and online journalism.
•	Volunteer Management Program — Structured volunteerism with training, recognition, and pathways to professional development.

9. Women Empowerment & Gender Equality
Climate change is not gender-neutral. Women and girls in Bangladesh face heightened climate risks, from increased domestic burden during disasters to loss of income, sexual violence in displacement, and exclusion from decision-making. YCN's gender programming is transformative and intersectional.

Key Programs:
•	Women's Climate Leadership Program — Building the capacity of women to lead climate action in their communities and beyond.
•	Women's Livelihood Groups — Forming and supporting women's self-help groups for climate-adaptive enterprise and savings.
•	Gender-Based Violence Prevention — Awareness and response programming in disaster-affected and vulnerable communities.
•	Girls' Education & Retention — Scholarships, mentoring, and community engagement to keep girls in school, particularly in climate-affected areas.
•	Women in Governance — Advocacy for meaningful representation of women in local government, disaster committees, and environmental bodies.

10. Social Harmony & Community Cohesion
Climate stress can intensify social tensions — over land, water, resources, and migration. YCN promotes social cohesion, interfaith harmony, and inclusive community development as essential foundations for resilience.

Key Programs:
•	Interfaith Climate Dialogues — Bringing together religious and community leaders to build shared values around environmental stewardship.
•	Youth Civic Engagement — Mobilizing young people in local governance, community decision-making, and social accountability processes.
•	Conflict-Sensitive Programming — Ensuring all YCN programs apply do-no-harm principles and contribute positively to social cohesion.
•	Cultural Programs for Climate Awareness — Using art, theater, music, and storytelling to communicate climate messages and celebrate community resilience.

 
11. Health, Nutrition & Community Well-being
Climate change is a health crisis. Flooding, heat waves, salinity intrusion, and displacement are driving surges in waterborne disease, malnutrition, mental illness, and maternal mortality across Bangladesh. YCN's health programming brings frontline preventive care and health education directly to vulnerable communities — particularly women, children, and the ultra-poor.

Key Programs:
•	Community Health Volunteer Network — Training and deploying community health volunteers for household visits, health education, and disease surveillance in program areas.
•	Maternal & Child Health — Antenatal care awareness, safe delivery promotion, immunization support, and newborn care education for mothers in climate-vulnerable communities.
•	Nutrition Programs — Addressing malnutrition among children under 5 and pregnant/lactating mothers through supplementation, dietary education, and kitchen garden promotion.
•	Adolescent Health & Reproductive Rights — Age-appropriate sexual and reproductive health education for adolescent girls and boys, including menstrual health, family planning awareness, and safe spaces.
•	Mental Health & Psychosocial Support — Community-based mental health first aid, stigma reduction campaigns, and referral systems for survivors of disasters, violence, and displacement.
•	Climate & Health Awareness — Community campaigns linking climate change to health outcomes — heatstroke, dengue, cholera, arsenic contamination — and building health-protective behaviors.
•	Disability-Inclusive Health — Ensuring people with disabilities access health services without discrimination, with targeted outreach and reasonable accommodation.

12. Financial Inclusion & Ultra-Poor Graduation
Poverty and climate vulnerability are deeply intertwined. Communities on the frontlines of climate change are often also the most economically marginalized — excluded from formal financial systems, unable to absorb shocks, and trapped in cycles of debt and destitution. YCN's financial inclusion and graduation programming provides a structured pathway out of extreme poverty, combining asset transfer, skills training, savings, and social support.

Key Programs:
•	Ultra-Poor Graduation Program — A time-bound, sequenced intervention combining asset transfer (productive assets such as livestock, seeds, or tools), business skills training, savings facilitation, healthcare linkage, and regular mentoring — enabling the ultra-poor to graduate to stable, dignified livelihoods.
•	Savings Groups & Village Organizations — Forming community savings and credit groups, particularly for women, enabling access to low-interest credit for productive investments and emergency needs.
•	Financial Literacy Training — Educating community members on budgeting, savings, loan management, and protection from predatory lending and money laundering.
•	Linkage to Formal Finance — Connecting graduates and program participants to mobile banking (bKash, Nagad), government social safety nets, and formal financial institutions.
•	Youth Entrepreneurship Fund — Small grants and mentoring for young entrepreneurs developing climate-smart businesses and social enterprises in underserved communities.
•	Social Enterprise Development — Supporting youth-led social enterprises that address local climate and development needs while generating sustainable income for reinvestment in community programs.

13. Legal Aid, Human Rights & Social Justice
Climate justice cannot be achieved without legal justice. Across Bangladesh, vulnerable communities — particularly women, minorities, indigenous peoples, and persons with disabilities — face land dispossession, discrimination, exploitation, and denial of rights in the context of climate displacement and environmental degradation. YCN provides legal literacy, rights-based education, and advocacy support to empower communities to claim and defend their rights.

Key Programs:
•	Legal Literacy Campaigns — Community workshops on land rights, women's rights under family and inheritance law, labor rights, and rights of climate-displaced persons.
•	Human Rights Documentation — Supporting communities in documenting rights violations, environmental harm, and climate injustice for advocacy and legal proceedings.
•	Access to Justice — Facilitating referrals to legal aid organizations and pro-bono lawyers for community members facing land grabbing, domestic violence, child marriage, and other violations.
•	Child Rights & Child Protection — Awareness and reporting mechanisms for child labor, child marriage, trafficking, and violence against children in climate-stressed communities.
•	Anti-Discrimination & Inclusion Advocacy — Advocacy for the rights of indigenous communities, char-land populations, and other marginalized groups facing structural exclusion.
•	Community Accountability Platforms — Supporting local social accountability mechanisms including citizen scorecards and community monitoring of public services and disaster relief.

14. Climate Migration, Displacement & Refugees
Bangladesh is at the epicenter of climate-induced displacement. Millions are already internally displaced due to riverbank erosion, flooding, and sea-level rise, and projections suggest tens of millions more may be forced to move by 2050. YCN addresses the rights, dignity, and resilience of climate migrants and displaced communities — both those compelled to move and those left behind.

Key Programs:
•	Climate Migration Mapping — Participatory documentation of migration patterns, drivers, and community impacts in climate hotspot areas including coastal belts, char lands, and haor regions.
•	Rights of the Displaced — Education and advocacy on the rights of internally displaced persons (IDPs), including access to shelter, education, healthcare, and livelihood entitlements.
•	Safe Migration Awareness — Information and orientation for youth and families considering migration, covering safe routes, legal status, rights at destination, and responsible remittance management.
•	Support for Receiving Communities — Programming to strengthen social cohesion, resource sharing, and equitable service access in urban and peri-urban areas receiving climate migrants.
•	Regional Advocacy on Climate Mobility — Representing Bangladesh's climate displacement crisis in South Asian and Asia Pacific forums, advocating for a just regional framework on climate mobility and refugee protection.

15. Knowledge Centers, Libraries & Community Learning
Access to knowledge is a fundamental right and a prerequisite for climate resilience. YCN establishes and supports community knowledge centers and libraries that serve as local hubs for learning, information, digital access, and civic engagement — directly inspired by BRAC's nationally transformative community library and learning center model.

Key Programs:
•	Community Climate Libraries — Establishing community reading rooms and resource centers stocked with books, periodicals, and educational materials on climate, environment, health, livelihoods, and rights in both Bangla and English.
•	Digital Access Centers — Providing computers, tablets, and internet connectivity in rural and peri-urban areas for digital literacy, e-learning, and access to online government services.
•	Climate Information Kiosks — Local weather, flood early warning, agricultural advisory, and government scheme information disseminated through community kiosks and public notice boards.
•	Youth Study Circles — Regular facilitated discussion and peer-learning groups for young people to explore climate science, civic issues, and sustainable development topics.
•	Community Radio & Local Media — Supporting community radio programming and local media production on climate, health, rights, livelihoods, and development topics in local dialects.

  GOVERNANCE & ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE  

Youth Climate Network operates under a democratic, youth-centered governance model that ensures accountability to its members, beneficiaries, and the broader public. The organizational structure is designed to enable rapid response to climate emergencies while maintaining strategic coherence and programmatic depth.

Governance Structure
•	General Assembly — The supreme decision-making body comprising all active YCN members. Meets annually to review strategy, elect leadership, and approve annual plans and budgets.
•	Executive Committee — Elected body of 7–11 youth leaders responsible for governance oversight, policy, and major institutional decisions.
•	Executive Director / Secretary General — Chief executive responsible for organizational management, external representation, and strategic leadership.
•	Program Teams — Dedicated teams for each of YCN's 10 strategic pillars, led by Program Coordinators.
•	Finance & Administration — Independent finance function with internal audit, transparent reporting, and donor accountability systems.
•	Youth Advisory Council — A representative body of young people from YCN's geographic areas of operation, ensuring grassroots perspectives inform all decisions.

Geographic Presence
•	National Headquarters: Bangladesh (Khulna division — coastal climate frontline)
•	Program presence across coastal, char, haor, and hill-tract regions of Bangladesh
•	Active representation at South Asian regional bodies and forums
•	Observer/participant status at Asia Pacific climate and development platforms
•	Networks with youth climate organizations across India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Philippines, and Pacific Island states
